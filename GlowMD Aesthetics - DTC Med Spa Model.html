Physician payment is built as (Work RVU × Work GPCI + Practice-Expense RVU × PE GPCI + Malpractice RVU × MP GPCI) × Conversion Factor — and it differs by facility vs. office setting and by geographic locality. Guide — what each input means

Service (the code)Each CPT/HCPCS code is a specific billable visit or service, and comes with three pre-set "relative value" weights (below). Pick the service you want to price.
Place of service — Office vs. FacilityOffice (non-facility): care in your own clinic, where you cover the overhead → higher practice-expense weight and usually a higher payment. Facility: hospital/surgery center, where the facility covers overhead and bills separately → lower physician payment.
Locality (GPCI)Geographic Practice Cost Index — a multiplier that adjusts each component for local costs (wages, rent, malpractice). Above 1.000 = a pricier area = higher pay; below 1.000 = lower. Miami's high malpractice GPCI (2.04), for example, lifts the MP piece.
Conversion factor (CF)The single national dollar amount CMS sets each year that turns total RVUs into dollars. The same CF applies to every code — change it and every rate moves together.
Work RVUThe physician's time, skill, and effort for the service — the "labor" component.
Practice-expense (PE) RVUOverhead — clinical staff, rent, supplies, and equipment. This is the piece that drops in a facility setting.
Malpractice (MP) RVUThe cost of liability insurance attributable to the service. Usually the smallest of the three.
Allowed amountWhat Medicare recognizes for the service. Medicare typically pays 80% and the patient or a secondary plan covers the remaining ~20%.

Illustrative. RVUs are approximate values from the CMS Medicare Physician Fee Schedule; the conversion factor and GPCIs are editable and set to round illustrative figures. Actual payment varies by performance year, exact locality, modifiers, and sequestration.
